Theorem 1.6.4. First fundamental theorem of calculus.

Let \(f\) be an integrable function on a closed interval \([a,b]\text{.}\) Let \(F\) be the function \([a,b]\) given by \(F(x) \defn \int_a^x f\text{.}\) Let \(a \leq c \leq b\text{.}\) If \(f\) is continuous at \(c\text{,}\) then \(F\) is differentiable at \(c\) and \(F'(c) =f(c)\text{.}\)
