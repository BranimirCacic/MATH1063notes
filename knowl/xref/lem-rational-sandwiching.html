Lemma 1.5.6.

Let \(x\) and \(y\) be real numbers satisfying \(y-x \gt 1\text{.}\) There exists \(k \in \Z\) such that \(x \lt k \lt y\text{.}\)

Proof.

By LemmaÂ 1.5.5, let \(N\) be the largest integer such that \(N \leq x\text{;}\) hence, set \(k \defn N+1\text{.}\) On the one hand, since \(N = \max\set{n \in \Z}{n \leq x}\) and \(k \gt N\text{,}\) it follows that \(k \gt x\text{.}\) On the other hand, since \(y-x \gt 1\) and \(N \leq x\text{,}\) it follow that \(y \gt 1+x \geq 1+N = k\text{.}\) Thus, we see that \(x \lt k \lt y\text{.}\)
